The Ningaloo Project modernized data collection for four of the six most
endangered sea turtle species in the world. The mobile app handled field
collection (geo-location, photo capture, offline data caching), and the web
dashboard handled the historical view, with sync between the two.

Working with marine biologists made me think about a question I've been
chasing ever since: what's the right tool for someone whose work happens
outside cell coverage and whose data has to survive imperfect sync?
Conservation tooling has a sharper definition of "reliable" than most
consumer software will ever need.
